# What is this?

This is a companion guide / strategy guide to the NASAA Series 65 Exam, otherwise known as the “Uniform Investment Adviser Law Exam”.

This guide is written for anyone looking to pass this exam as a pre-requisite to invest in pre-IPO startups as an individual angel investor.

Because the exam was originally intended to license Investment Adviser Representatives (IARs), test prep resources you may find online are geared towards finance professionals. This guide hopefully helps to fill in any conceptual gaps you might encounter while studying for this exam, if your professional background is not in finance.

This guide is applicable to anyone looking to angel invest in U.S.-based startups, and don’t otherwise meet the other qualifications to be considered an “accredited investor”. Note: even if you’re a foreign investor, these still apply to you under United States securities laws.

# How to use this site

This site is organized as a digital garden: all notes are linked to each other and shown in an interactive graph visualization. If you’re familiar with Roam Research, Obisidian, RemNote, or LogSeq, you’ll feel immediately at home.

The best way to browse this site is to click on any link that interests you!

However, if you’re a returning user, or if you prefer browsing the contents of this site through a structured list, here’s a list of all notes under each category:

  • 📚 Guides — Tips to get started on your journey to pass the Series 65 exam

  • 🗄 Resources — Specific resources (books, courses, videos) that will help you in understanding and mastering the material

  • 🔖 Topics — A conceptual walkthrough of the various topics that will be covered in the exam, and how they’re related to each other

  • 💭 Concepts — Concepts that are covered across different topics and question in the exam
